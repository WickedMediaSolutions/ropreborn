## Welcome to Rites of Passage

You're playing the **Rites of Passage (RoP)** conversion of ROM 2.4, a hardcore PvP-focused MUD designed to challenge players and create meaningful character journeys.

**Core Philosophy**: Every action matters. Every death costs something. Every kill advances your legend.

## Character Creation: Your First Choice Matters

When creating a character, you make **two permanent decisions**:

### 1. Choose Your Profession (Class)

Nine professions available - pick the one that resonates:

#### **Warrior** - The Soldier
- **Strength**: Highest melee damage, heavy armor mastery, no mana cost
- **Weakness**: Low mobility, limited crowd control
- **Playstyle**: Tank up and smash. Best for group fights
- **Spec**: Gets **Parry** and **Block** passives (reduce incoming damage)
- **Recommended gear**: Full plate armor, 2-handed weapon

#### **Cleric** - The Healer
- **Strength**: Healing spells, protection magic, resurrection
- **Weakness**: Low damage output, vulnerable to interrupts
- **Playstyle**: Keep yourself and allies alive. Solo is slow
- **Spec**: **Heal** (instant, high mana cost), **Holy Shield** (damage reduction)
- **Recommended gear**: Holy symbol, light armor, shield

#### **Templar** - The Hybrid
- **Strength**: Balanced healing + tanking, decent damage
- **Weakness**: Master of none, specialized players outperform
- **Playstyle**: Flexible solo and group, smooth progression
- **Spec**: **Crisis Heal** (group heal), **Smite** (damage + healing)
- **Recommended gear**: Balanced armor + weapon, holy symbol

#### **Monk** - The Martial Artist
- **Strength**: High crit rate, evasion, unarmed damage
- **Weakness**: Low health pool, needs careful positioning
- **Playstyle**: Strike fast, dodge incoming fire, kite enemies
- **Spec**: **Whirlwind** (attack twice), **Evasion** (dodge passive)
- **Recommended gear**: Light armor, hand wraps, speed boots

#### **Warlock** - The Dark Mage
- **Strength**: Crowd control, DoT damage, curses
- **Weakness**: Slow damage ramp, requires mana management
- **Playstyle**: Disable enemies while DoTs tick. Complex but powerful
- **Spec**: **Curse** (reduce enemy stats), **Drain** (life steal)
- **Recommended gear**: Robes, staff, mana regen items

#### **Thief** - The Rogue
- **Strength**: Burst damage, stealth, backstab combos
- **Weakness**: High risk, detected alts get punished
- **Playstyle**: Hit hard and hide. Timing is everything
- **Spec**: **Backstab** (2x damage from behind), **Stealth** (invisibility)
- **Recommended gear**: Leather, daggers, shadow items

#### **Ninja** - The Assassin
- **Strength**: Movement speed, agility, shadow techniques
- **Weakness**: Needs gear to be effective
- **Playstyle**: Circle enemies, dodge attacks, execute targets
- **Spec**: **Shadow Clone** (temporary duplicate), **Storm Step** (mobility)
- **Recommended gear**: Dark leather, throwing weapons, speed items

#### **Mage** - The Elementalist
- **Strength**: Area damage, crowd control, versatility
- **Weakness**: Slowest leveling early on
- **Playstyle**: Control the battlefield with AoE spells
- **Spec**: **Meteor** (2-3 target damage), **Frost Nova** (freeze area)
- **Recommended gear**: Robes, staff, intelligence items

#### **Alchemist** - The Support
- **Strength**: Buff allies, create consumables, utility
- **Weakness**: No direct damage threat
- **Playstyle**: Enable others to win (group play essential)
- **Spec**: **Haste** (speed buff), **Transmute** (craft items)
- **Recommended gear**: Robes, alchemical components, stat stacking

## Leveling: Your First 50 Levels

### Levels 1-10 (Newbie Zone)
- **Duration**: 5-10 hours of active play
- **Area**: Midgaard Plains (safe area, no PK combat)
- **Mobs**: Rabbits (level 1), Goblins (level 3-5), Gnomes (level 5-8)
- **Goals**:
  - Learn your profession's basic spells/skills
  - Get comfortable with combat commands
  - Gather basic equipment (vendor items)
  - Establish key hotkeys for abilities

**Pro tip**: Don't worry about optimization. Explore, learn the map, get comfortable.

### Levels 10-20 (Early Game)
- **Duration**: 10-15 hours
- **Areas**: Eastern Woods, Marshland, Catacombs
- **Mobs**: Orks (10-12), Spiders (12-15), Skeletal undead (15-18)
- **Goals**:
  - Master your profession's core spells
  - Farm basic equipment (weapon/armor upgrades)
  - Try your first PvP (warpoint enabled here)
  - Find a group/clan if interested

**Pro tip**: Your first death hurts. You'll lose 10% of your level XP and drop equipped items. Plan fights carefully.

### Levels 20-35 (Mid Game)
- **Duration**: 15-20 hours per 5 levels
- **Areas**: Draconia, Wyvern Tower, Moria mines
- **Mobs**: Dragonkin (20-25), Dwarven warriors (25-30), Ancient golems (30-35)
- **Goals**:
  - Acquire "rare" equipment (quest rewards, boss drops)
  - Specialize your build (damage vs survival vs healing)
  - Engage in organized PvP (group battles, territory control)
  - Farm warpoints for Novice → Adept rank

**Pro tip**: Your Sect matters now. Join your alignment's stronghold, learn alignment-specific spells.

### Levels 35-50 (End Game)
- **Duration**: 20-30 hours per 5 levels
- **Areas**: Olympus, Nirvana, Astral planes
- **Mobs**: Ancient dragons (35-40), Celestial beings (40-45), Planar entities (45-50)
- **Goals**:
  - Chase "epic" equipment (legendary quality, unique abilities)
  - Master advanced combat tactics (spell combos, positioning)
  - Dominate PvP (Warpoint rank progression to Legend tier)
  - Explore 50-hour goal achievement celebration

**Pro tip**: At level 50, you have choices - stay and dominate PvP, or remort to start over with bonuses.

## The Warpoint System: Your PvP Legacy

### What are Warpoints?

**Warpoints** measure your PvP prowess and legendary status. Kill other players, gain warpoints. Warpoints determine your rank.

### How to Earn Warpoints

**Base gains**:
- Kill any player → +10 warpoints
- Kill player with 500+ warpoints → +15 warpoints (victim's worth matters)
- Kill player 5+ levels below you → +5 warpoints (level bonus reduced)
- Kill opposite alignment → +5 warpoints bonus (good vs evil)

**Seasonal multipliers** (only apply to base gain):
- Spring War (March): 1.5x all warpoint gains (warpoint economy heat increases)
- Summer Summit (June): 1.0x (normal season)
- Fall Clash (September): 1.25x (slightly boosted)
- Winter Solstice (December): 1.2x (holiday spikes)

**Anti-Farming Protection**:
- Kill same player 3x in 30 min → warpoint gain cut in half
- Kill same player 5x in 1 hour → get 0 warpoints for 1 hour (protection window)
- Prevents griefing and alt farming exploits

### Warpoint Ranks

Work your way up the ladder:

| Rank | Warpoint Range | Effort | Status |
|------|-----------------|--------|---------|
| Novice | 0-99 | ~10 kills | Learning PvP |
| Adept | 100-499 | ~50 kills | Serious threat |
| Veteran | 500-999 | ~100 kills | Elite tier |
| Champion | 1000-2499 | ~250 kills | Top player |
| Legend | 2500+ | 500+ kills | Server legend |

**Progression example**:
- Start at 0 warpoints
- Kill 10 players (50-100 warpoints at base gain)
- Reach Adept rank (easier, more players respect you)
- Grind 50 more kills (500 total warpoints)
- Reach Veteran (now you appear in top-10 leaderboard)
- Push to Champion over weeks of PvP

### Warpoint Decay & Leadership

Warpoints never decay (they're permanent achievements). Your legend persists forever.

**Leaderboard** (check with `score` command):
- Top-5 players displayed in public leaderboard
- Server resets count, warpoints persist through character progression

## Death: The Ultimate Penalty

### What Happens When You Die

**XP Loss** (permanent):
- Lose 10% of your level's XP requirement
- You do NOT level-down (stay at level 49 with reduced XP bar)
- Motivates careful play at high levels
- Carries through logout/login (XP loss persists)

**Item Loss** (up to 1 hour):
- **Equipped items** (armor, weapons, carried gear): 100% go to a corpse
- **Inventory items**: 50% to corpse, 50% to "death bag"
- **Death bag**: Protected items, can't drop/sell/trade them
- **Corpse decay**: 60 minutes later, unpicked-up items disappear forever

### Corpse Recovery

**Process**:
1. Die and lose items
2. Corpse appears at death location
3. **First 10 minutes**: Only you can loot the corpse
4. **After 10 minutes**: Anyone can loot the corpse (PvP consequence)
5. **After 60 minutes**: Corpse disappears, items lost forever

**Strategy**:
- Die in safe zone? Easy recovery
- Die in PvP hotspot? Race to corpse before enemies loot it
- Die with 10 potions? 5 in corpse (lost if not retrieved), 5 in death bag (safe)

**Death bag access**:
- Open `death bag` command to see protected items
- These items are yours forever, can't be stolen
- Disappear when you loot full corpse

### Strategic Implications

- **High-level PvP risk**: Lose rare equipment + 10% level progress
- **Encourages caution**: Don't wade into fights with full gear
- **Enables recovery**: Death bag ensures you keep some resources
- **Time pressure**: 1 hour window to recover corpse creates drama

## The Remort System: Restart with Bonuses

### What is a Remort?

**Remort** = Reset your level to 1, but keep permanent benefits

After reaching level 50, you can choose to remort and start the leveling journey again.

### Remort Benefits

On remort (each cycle):
- **+50 Max HP** (carried forever)
- **+50 Max Move** (carried forever)
- **+2 Skill Slots** (learn 2 more spells/abilities)
- **Hunger/Thirst eliminated** (no need to eat/drink)
- **+1 to all stats** noticeably (higher damage, AC, etc.)

### Remort Progression

| Remort # | Max HP Bonus | Skill Slots | Time to level 50 |
|----------|--------------|-------------|------------------|
| 1st | +50 | +2 | 50-100 hours |
| 2nd | +100 | +4 | 5-10 hours (boosted) |
| 3rd | +150 | +6 | 3-5 hours (much faster) |
| 4th+ | +50x | +2x | Hours decrease per remorrt |

**Example progression**:
- Level 1-50: 75 hours (average player, normal difficulty)
- Remort 1 (now level 1 again): 8 hours to level 50 (bonuses help)
- Remort 2: 5 hours to level 50 (easier mobs, better stats)
- Remort 3: 3 hours to level 50 (veteran player speed)

### Strategy

- **First remort**: Marks transition to "serious player" (8 hours is achievable goal)
- **Multiple remorts**: Hardcore players do 5-10+ remorts for stat capping
- **Remorting resets warpoints? NO**: Warpoints carry across remorting permanently

## Sect Spells & Powers

### Accessing Sect Magic

When you reach **level 20**, your sect unlocks its first spell:

**Good Sects**:
- Aethelhelm: **Divine Healing** (instant heal)
- Kiri: **Nature's Blessing** (heal + buff)
- Baalzom: **Celestial Intervention** (group heal)
- Ishta: **Serenity** (healing focus spell)

**Evil Sects**:
- Zod: **Dark Bolt** (instant damage)
- Jalaal: **Drain Touch** (damage + life steal)
- Xix: **Shadow Curse** (DoT + reduce enemy stats)
- Talice: **Entropy** (cumulative damage over time)

### Sect-Specific Mechanics

**Good alignment benefits**:
- Healing in good temples boosted 50%
- Protection from evil magic (reduce dark damage 5-15%)
- Stat bonus in good-aligned areas (+1 to primary stat)

**Evil alignment benefits**:
- Damage in evil strongholds boosted 15%
- Stat bonus in evil areas (+1 primary stat)
- Life steal mechanics work better (heal 15% more from drain skills)

### The Good vs Evil Conflict

**When you PK**:
- Good kills Evil: Warpoint bonus +5 (good side being rewarded)
- Evil kills Good: Warpoint bonus +5 (evil side being rewarded)
- Same alignment kills: No bonus (internal conflict discouraged)

**Consequence**:
- Encourages good vs evil warfare
- Discourages same-faction PK grief
- Creates natural team alignments

## Advanced Topics

### Group Dynamics

**How grouping works**:
- Join party: 75% XP per member (you + ally get 3 members = 75% each)
- Shared loot pool (need per item)
- Shared experience from kills
- Shared death consequences (you don't lose XP for their deaths)

**Optimal party**:
- 1 Tank (Warrior)
- 1 Healer (Cleric/Templar)
- 1-2 DPS (Thief, Mage, Monk)
- 1 Support (Alchemist buffs, Warlock crowd control)

### PvP Tactics

**Positioning**:
- Casters should stand back (range advantage)
- Healers behind the line (protect from focus fire)
- Tanks in front (soak damage)

**Timing**:
- Burst-win: Use cooldowns together for quick kills
- Sustain-win: Healers keep allies up while DPS grinds enemy down
- Control-win: Disable enemies (freeze, stun) and nuke

**Alignment warfare**:
- Good teams leverage healing + protection
- Evil teams leverage damage amp + crowd control
- Battles snowball - first kill can swing entire fight

### Economy & Trading

**What's valuable**:
- Rare gear (drops from boss mobs)
- Potions (healing resources)
- Spell books (learn new abilities)
- Warpoint tokens (if tradeable)

**Trading**:
- Use `offer <item>` and `accept` to trade safely
- Can't trade death bag items (protected)
- Can't trade player-owned houses or properties

## Troubleshooting Player Issues

### "I died and can't find my corpse"

**Steps**:
1. `where [character_name]` if you remember death location
2. Check `inventory` - do you see death bag items?
3. Go back to death location - corpse should be there
4. If 60 minutes passed - corpse is gone, items lost forever

**Recovery**: Go to Ishta (good) or Jalaal (evil) temples for resurrection NPC.

### "My XP loss persists after logout"

**Expected behavior** - XP loss is PERMANENT. It carries across sessions.

**Recovery**: You must re-gain that XP (grind or quest) to re-level.

### "I got frozen/banned but I didn't cheat"

**Contact immortals**: Email admin or send tell to on-duty immortal.

**Appeal process**: Admins review logs and may unfreeze if mistaken.

**Cause debugging**: Check ADMIN_GUIDE.md "Denied Access Recovery" section.

### "My sect spell isn't working"

**Checklist**:
1. Are you level 20+? (spell unlocks at 20)
2. Is your alignment locked to your sect?
3. Do you have enough mana? (check `score`)
4. Is the entity you're targeting valid?

**Example**: `cast divine_healing` (if Aethelhelm) or `cast dark_bolt` (if Zod)
